Chemical Engineering Transfer Pathway

Ensure a seamless transition into the department by completing your foundational coursework before your first Fall semester.

Important Transfer Note: To stay on track for a timely graduation, Chemical Engineering curriculum sequences strictly require specific prerequisites to enroll in junior-level core design courses in the Fall. Missing foundational courses can delay graduation by a full academic year.

Suggested Course Progression

Stage 1: Introductory Foundations

Typically completed in the first two semesters at your community college.

  • Calculus I & II (Differential & Integral)
  • General Chemistry I & II (with Labs)
  • Engineering Mechanics (Statics/Physics I)

Stage 2: Core Mathematics & Sciences

Typically completed by the third semester.

  • Calculus III (Multivariable Calculus)
  • Organic Chemistry I (with Lab)
  • Physics II (Electricity & Magnetism)

Stage 3: Pre-Transfer Finalization

Critical completion target during your final semester before transfer.

  • Differential Equations (Linear Algebra combined preferred)
  • Organic Chemistry II (Highly recommended)
  • Intro to Material & Energy Balances (If available via cross-enrollment)

Stage 4: University Arrival (Fall Entry)

Your baseline starting point upon official matriculation.

  • Ready for: Chemical Engineering Thermodynamics
  • Ready for: Fluid Mechanics / Transport Phenomena
  • Ready for: Unit Operations Laboratory
